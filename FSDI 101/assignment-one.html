Biography

I'm from Santa Ana, California, and I grew up in Phoenix, Arizona. I joined the US Army in 2014 and got out in 2017. I was medically discharged due to injuries to my back and hips. My original plan for after the Army was to try and join a law enforcement agency like the US Marshalls or something like that. Due to my injuries I was unable to pursue that path, and that's alright.

What I've been working up to since my separation from the military, is acquiring as many skills as possible to put together, and open, a custom vehicle fab/repair shop. What has happened so far is that I've completed a combination welding program from a trade school in Arizona, and upon graduation from that school I began working in the metal trades; more specifically, in the pipe trades as a pipefitter/welder. In late 2019 I went back to the sametrade school and completed the diesel heavy truck program they offer, which has helped expand my knowledge about both gasoline and diesel engine technologies. I feel that course has been an immense help to get me closer to the ultimate goal.

My latest job change has me working for Mr. Elon Musk building nosecones for the Starship project. I started with SpaceX as a TIG welder in November of 2020 during the covid pandemic. Two short months later I became a lead with my own team. My job made me realize that I'm not going to be able to work in this field forever. Maybe as a hobby, but not at the normally required workpace of a place like SpaceX.

I rarely go on Snapchat, but for some reason I did, once, maybe two weeks ago. I saw a buddy of mine had posted a story of a schoolwork project he was working on. He had built a welcome page for a website with elements I had never given a second thought to until that moment. I asked him about it and he said it was web development, and that he was coding. I didn't give it much thought in the moment, but later on, I had an itch that needed to be scratched. So I typed "coding app" into the search bar on the play store, and from there I fell down the rabbit hole. This is the career path I want to take in order to allow me enough income and time for other things needed to set up my shop. And so, we're off.
